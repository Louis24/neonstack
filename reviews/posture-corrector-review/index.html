Frequently Asked Questions

Do posture correctors actually work long-term?

Posture correctors provide temporary alignment but don't strengthen muscles. For lasting improvement, combine brace use (20-30 min daily) with back strengthening exercises. Think of braces as training wheels, not a permanent solution.

What's better: lumbar cushion or posture brace?

Lumbar cushions are more comfortable for all-day use and support your lower back where pain typically originates. Posture braces correct shoulder slouching but become uncomfortable after 2+ hours. Use lumbar cushions daily, braces occasionally.

Can I sleep in a posture corrector?

No. Never sleep in posture correctors - they restrict movement and breathing during sleep. Use them only while sitting or standing. For sleep posture, try a cervical pillow or body pillow instead.

How thick should a lumbar cushion be?

4-5 inches thick works for most people. Too thin (under 3") provides inadequate support. Too thick (over 6") pushes you forward uncomfortably. Adjust your chair's backrest angle if the cushion feels too prominent.
