Frequently Asked Questions

Do blue light glasses actually work?

Yes, for reducing eye strain and improving sleep when used at night. However, they do NOT prevent myopia progression or cure dry eye syndrome. The 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds) remains the best eye protection method.

What blocking rate should I choose?

For daytime work: 30-40% (nearly clear lenses). For long coding/design sessions: 50-60% (light yellow tint). For night use before bed: 80-95% (amber lenses) to maximize melatonin production.

Can I add blue light blocking to my prescription glasses?

Yes. Most optical shops offer blue light coating as an add-on for $20-$40. This is often more cost-effective than buying separate computer glasses.

Are software solutions like Night Shift enough?

Software filters (Night Shift, f.lux) only affect your screens. Blue light glasses protect from all light sources including ambient lighting. The best approach combines both: software filters + low-blocking glasses.
